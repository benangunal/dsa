How it works

📋 Instructions

1
Open in Google Colab

Click one of the ▶ in Colab buttons above. The notebook opens directly in your browser — no installation needed. Sign in with any Google account.

2
Enter your student ID

Replace "12345678" with your real student ID in the first cell and run it. Your ID seeds a unique personal dataset — every student gets different signal parameters, fault positions, and hidden values. Use your real ID: two identical reports means one is copied.

3
Run all cells top to bottom

The notebook is fully written — just run each cell in order and read the code and comments as you go. The final cell prints your personal engineering report automatically. Make sure every cell has run and all outputs are visible before submitting.

4
Submit the notebook on Blackboard before the deadline

Download your completed notebook via File → Download → Download .ipynb and upload it to the Blackboard assignment. The notebook already contains your student ID, dataset seed, all outputs, and your written reflection — everything in one file. Make sure all cells have been run. Resubmission is allowed; only the last upload counts.

5
Present live next class — the day after the deadline

You will be called to open your notebook and walk through it step by step. Be ready to explain what every function does, why it produces your specific numbers, and what the results mean for the engineering scenario. Students who cannot explain the code receive zero — regardless of whether it runs.

6
AI tools are allowed

You may use Claude, ChatGPT, Copilot, or any other assistant. Use them to understand the code — not to skip understanding it. The live presentation will immediately reveal the difference, and your dataset is unique so your numbers are yours alone.
